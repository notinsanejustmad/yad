Word order

The general word order in yad is SOV, but it can be changed to OVS to mark passive voice. Adjectives come after nouns, and adverbs after verbs. Tense is marked with particles before the verb, and conjunctions generally come in the first position. The indirect object always comes after the verb.

Adjectives and adverbs

Any adjective in yad can be used as an adverb and vice versa. Adjectives in yad inflect for gender and are positioned directly after the noun the describe. Adverbs agree in gender with the verb.

on isyela isye-net
CLF cat silent-N
"The silent cat"

Adverbs on the other hand are positioned directly behind the verb and agree on gender with the verb.

on isyela ow-et isye-net
CLF cat travel-N cat-N
"The cat travels silently"

Subordinate clauses and indirect objects

Subordinate clauses instead of having a marked Absolutive mark Nominative, and they appear after the main clause. Subclauses, when not being preceded by another particle like naq “because” are constructed by inserting na in front of the subclause.

on isyela na i-far ghim-et ow-et
CLF cat REL 1SG-NOM love-N travel-N
"The cat that loves me travels"
ild isye naq ild-ar isyela falid-et
3SG silent because 3SG-NOM cat be-N
"They are silent because they are a cat"




Indirect objects are inserted into a clause using a subordinate clause right after the verb.

iisyela-niow-icnabaogh-ar
1SGcat-ABStravel-FRELCLFsun-NOM
"I take the cat to church"

Questions

A question is formed by inserting the word khos in the position of the word that is asked for. Also, for some questions it takes a classifier. Khos inflects like a noun of the abstract gender.
Alternatively a yes/no question is formed by inserting yat. Yes/no questions are answered by repeating the word in question: "Did the cat travel?" - "The cat did travel/didn't travel".
