Morphology

Jad is an agglutinative language with isolating parts and a tendency towards suffixed, with the evidentials being the only prefixes.

The copula

The copula in yad is don and can be roughly translated to english "to be". It has a variety of uses, but mainly it is use to denote that the subject is the object. It is the only verb that can take adjectives as object, as seen in the following examples.

onisyelaisye-nidon-oj
CLF:animalcatsilent-ABSCOP-A
"The cat is silent"
zin koraisyela-ninefdon-et
Koracat-ABSNEGCOP-N
"Kora is not a cat"

Noun Cases

Jad has seven cases:
Ergative, Absolutive, Nominative, Possessive, Commitative, Ornative and Locative.
The order of these cases is ERG/ABS/NOM-POS-COM-LOC-ORN.

Ergative

The subject of a verb in a transitive sentence takes the ergative case, which remains unmarked. It also marks the subject on a reflexive verb.

i-∅onisyela-nighim-ic
1SG-ERGCLF:animalcat-ABSlove-F
"I love the cat"
i-∅cor-ic
1SG-ERGspeak-F
"I encourage myself"

Absolutive

The absolutive case marks the subject of an intransitive verb or the object of a transitive verb. It is marked by the suffix -ni. The /n/ is dropped after nasals and unvoiced consonants.

iisyela-nighim-ic
1SGcat-ABSlove-F
"I love the cat"
i-nicor-ic
1SG-ABSspeak-F
"I speak"

Nominative

The nominative marks the subject of an transitive or intransitive subordinate sentence. The suffix for this case is -far. The /f/ is dropped after stops, nasals, /f/ and /v/.

ionisyela-nighim-icsemild-arighim-ojnef
1SGCLF:animalcat-ABSlove-Fbut3SG-NOM1SGlove-ANEG
"I love the cat but it doesn't love me"
ionisyela-nighim-icsemild-arow-kaj
1SGCLF:animalcat-ABSlove-Fbut3SG-NOMtravel-A
"I love the cat but it runs (away)"

Possessive

The possessive case marks the possessor of something (just like 's in english). The suffix is -ja. /i/ and /e/ are dropped if a word marked by this case ends in them. This includes the 1st person singular i. Noun possessed by the possessor can't have an article.

jaisyela
1SG.POScat
"My cat"

Comitative

The comitative is used to express something similiar to the english word "with" and is marked by the suffix -dog, although the /d/ is dropped after consonants that aren't approximants. The comitative case can also function as a Instrumental case. If the noun marked with comitative is a subject and has a different grammatical gender than the non-marked subject, the verb inflects as if it had a neuter subject.

iisyela-dogow-et
1SGcat-COMtravel-N
"I travel with a cat"
iifela-dogife-t
1SGknife-COMcut-N
"I cut with the knife"

Locative

The locative serves the purpose of expressing that a given noun is in, on, or around another noun. It uses the suffix -ko with the /k/ being dropped after stops, uvular fricatives and /m/.

zinzolaonisyela-kodon-et
CLF:peoplekidCLF:animalcat-LOCCOP-N
"The kid is on the cat"

Ornative

The Ornative is used to denote that X is worn by Y (ornates it). It uses -ica as suffix, although the /i/ is dropped after another vowel. If the root word ends in /a/, that /a/ is dropped instead of the /i/.
This case is like a passive version of the Locative case, since the "agent", or the thing ornating is after the "ornatee".

bakhemeonisyel-ica
CLF:placetowerCLF:animalcat-ORN
"The cat is on the tower (lit. The tower is ornated by the cat)"

Grammatical gender and Noun classes

Jad has 5 grammatical genders, aswell as 6 specific noun classes. The genders are Inanimate, Masculine, Feminine, Neuter, Abstract, and they are (normally) assigned based on the ending of the word. There also are noun classes for Places, People, Plants, Masses of water, Animals, and Non-visible things; but the Inflections for the grammatical gender are kept. The definite articles differs for each of the genders and dictionary entries show these, as well as the noun classes a word can take.

Inanimate

The inanimate gender occurs on words with the endings -e/-n/-z and its definite article is zoq. Verbs and adjectives agreeing with inanimate words take the suffix -tan where the /t/ is dropped after /t c d g m ç v ɣ/.

zoqkhemexoe-tan
DET.INANtowertall-INAN
"The tall tower"

Masculine

The masculine occurs on words with the endings -gh/-m/-s/-je and its definite article is ka. Verbs and adjectives agreeing with masculine verbs take the suffix -wag where the /w/ is dropped after /a/ and approximants. If the word ends with /u/ the /u/ is deleted.

village
kadayalimwije-wag
DET.Mimportant-M
"The important village"

Feminine

The feminine occurs on words with the ending -i and its definite article is si. Verbs and adjectives agreeing with feminine verbs take the suffix -ic.

sicisi-ninogho-ic
DET.Feye-ABSsee-F
"The eye sees"

Neuter

The neuter occurs on words with the endings -l/-o/-d/-tla/-ja and its definite article is ki. Verbs and adjecitves agreeing with neuter verbs take the suffix -net where the /n/ is dropped after nasals, unvoiced consonants and /w/.
