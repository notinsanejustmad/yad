Allophony

All consonants are devoiced word finally. /l/ an /w/ both become the unvoiced lateral fricative when word final (/w/ coming from the earlier voiced version of this).

Before uvulars, /i/ is realized as [ɪ]. Also, after uvulars /i e a/ become [ɯ ɜ ɑ] respectively.

Prosody

Stressed syllables, while predictable and non-phonemic, vary in loudness (they are louder) and in pitch (they are higher). Non-content words are often left without or just with secondary pitch and seem to be connected to the next word if it begins in a stressed syllable. Syllables before stressed syllables have an increase in pitch, but the pitch drops suddenly after the stressed syllable.
