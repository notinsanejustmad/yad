Darkness Outside

Its dark outside, I can't see far. It seems as if the world is reduced to just here, the darkness outside swallowing everything else.
Inside, illuminated only by the flickering glow of my candle, is the polar opposite of the dark cosmos outside, my light acting as a shield to the outside world. Who knows if there's anything sentient out there, maybe I'm the only one who can think about this.
This room is cramped. There's not much here physically, yet the entire room is filled with ghosts: ideas, memories and all sorts of thoughts flying through the empty space. They enjoy my presence and I treasure theirs. It may seem as if they have infested this space, robbing me of my space and freedom. This is not entirely true. They might do that but I don't want to interrupt them, I value what they give me. Little specters from the past, the present and of what will be.
I continue writing, I don't want to lose what they give me just yet, but soon I will run out of paper and ink, we'll have to think of something else.
Is it just me or did I just see a light out there? I could've sworn I saw the fire of another candle in the darkness. But I can't give myself false hopes. It isn't good for me to look into this darkness, I'll end up disappointed. And of what use is it if I have enough company in here?
And just like that I keep writing, knowingly oblivious of what might be, everything reduced to my small world.
